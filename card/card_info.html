Card Information

Card 1: Ace of Hearts

The Ace of Hearts is often considered the highest card in the suit of Hearts. In many games, it can be played as either high (above King) or low (below 2).

Card 2: King of Spades

The King of Spades is often called the 'Suicide King' because he appears to be sticking his sword behind his head. It's one of the highest-value cards in many games.

Card 3: Queen of Diamonds

The Queen of Diamonds is sometimes referred to as the 'Queen of Avarice'. In the game of Hearts, this card is often used as a special scoring card.

Card 4: Jack of Clubs

The Jack of Clubs is also known as the 'One-eyed Jack' because it's typically depicted in profile, showing only one eye. It's often used in poker as a wild card.

Card 5: Ten of Hearts

The Ten of Hearts is part of the royal flush in poker when combined with the Jack, Queen, King, and Ace of Hearts. It's also a valuable card in the game of Hearts.

Card 6: Nine of Spades

The Nine of Spades is sometimes called the 'Curse of Scotland'. In some variations of Poker, it can be used as a wild card.

Card 7: Eight of Diamonds

The Eight of Diamonds is part of the 'snowman' in poker (8♦8♠). In the game of Crazy Eights, it allows the player to change the suit.

Card 8: Seven of Clubs

The Seven of Clubs is considered lucky in some cultures. In the game of Septica, sevens are the highest cards.

Card 9: Six of Hearts

The Six of Hearts is part of the 'Devil's Bedpost' hand in poker (four sixes). In some trick-taking games, it can be a valuable card for making books.

Card 10: Five of Spades

The Five of Spades is part of the 'Poker' hand in the game of Cribbage. In Poker, five cards of the same suit (including the Five of Spades) form a flush.

Card 11: Four of Diamonds

The Four of Diamonds is part of the 'Little Joe' in craps (a hard four). In card counting systems for Blackjack, it's often assigned a positive value.

Card 12: Three of Clubs

The Three of Clubs is the lowest club in many trick-taking games. In some variations of Poker, three threes are called 'crabs'.
